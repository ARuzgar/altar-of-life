# Health System

> Health is not a safety buffer. It is the currency of ambition.

## Core Principle

In Altar of Life, **health is the primary resource**. Players do not spend gold, coins, or abstract points — they spend their own vitality. This creates a constant existential tension: every purchase is a gamble on survival.

Being "wealthy" in this game means being low on health. Power and fragility are the same thing.

## How Health Is Spent

### 1. Bidding (Primary Sink)
Players spend health to acquire cards via the [[Bidding System]]. The amount bid is permanently lost — there is no refund or change.

### 2. Health-Drain Cards (Planned)
Certain powerful cards will cost health directly to activate or sustain — separate from the bidding phase. Examples:
- A card that deals massive damage but drains X health on play
- A summon that passively drains health each round it remains in play
- An ability that lets you "overcharge" your turn by sacrificing health for extra mana or card draws

### 3. Overcharge Mechanic (Planned)
Players may be able to voluntarily spend health during their turn to power up an action — essentially borrowing against their own survival. Details TBD.

*See [[Future Considerations]] for design notes on health-drain timing and balance.*

## Elimination

A player whose health reaches **0 is eliminated**. The last player alive wins.

## Design Notes

- The absence of a "safe" resource creates pressure on every decision
- Players with low health are simultaneously the most powerful bidders and the most vulnerable targets — this asymmetry should generate interesting threat dynamics in multiplayer
- Avoid making health restoration too accessible early; it undercuts the core tension
