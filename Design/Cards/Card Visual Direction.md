# Card Visual Direction

> The cards should feel like they were found, not printed.

## Core Aesthetic

**Ethereal Modern Tarot** — a synthesis of:

- Classical tarot card composition and symbolism
- Constellation and celestial cartography
- Holographic / foil printing techniques
- Contemporary minimalist line art
- The weight and ritual feeling of objects with history

The cards are **black ground** with **gold, silver, and holographic foil** line work. They feel expensive, sacred, and slightly dangerous to hold. They look like they belong on an altar.

## Design Principles

**1. Black ground as sacred space**
The blackness is not emptiness — it is the void between worlds. Every line drawn on it emerges from that dark. This should feel deliberate, not incidental.

**2. Foil as life force**
The gold and holographic elements should feel like something *alive* caught in the card — soul energy made visible. Holographic foil in particular catches light unpredictably, making each viewing slightly different. The cards should feel like they respond to being looked at.

**3. Constellation geometry**
Illustrations should have an underlying geometric logic — characters and figures built from or surrounded by star-map linework. Points connected by fine lines, suggesting that everything here is mapped by some larger pattern.

**4. Typographic restraint**
Card names in clean, elegant serif or transitional typefaces. No clutter. The card name should feel like a pronouncement, not a label.

**5. Type-coded color language**
Each mana school (TBD) should have an associated foil color:
- Shadow → deep violet / black foil
- Flame → copper / amber
- Verdant → green / gold
- Aether → silver / iridescent
- *(other types TBD)*

This allows players to identify card types at a glance while maintaining aesthetic unity.

## What to Avoid

- Busy, cluttered illustrations — the black ground needs room to breathe
- Bright, saturated color fills — this is a foil-on-black world, not watercolor
- Generic fantasy art conventions (glowing swords, spell circles, etc.) — we want tarot symbolism, not MMO aesthetics
- Digital-looking gradients — even in digital versions, the aesthetic should reference physical printing techniques

## Inspirations (Art Direction)

- The Thoth Tarot (Aleister Crowley / Lady Frieda Harris) — geometric, esoteric, golden
- Rider-Waite revisionist decks — modern reinterpretations preserving symbolic density
- Klimt's gold leaf work — *see [[Inspirations]]*
- Astronomical charts and star atlases (Johannes Hevelius, Uranometria)
- Alchemy manuscripts and their diagrams

## Lore Portal — QR Code & Deep Link

Each physical card will carry a **QR code** in a discreet location (back of card, corner, or worked into the border design). Each digital card in the video game will carry an equivalent **tap-to-reveal link**.

These lead to a lore entry specific to that card — a fragment of story, a myth, a memory. Not rules text. Pure lore.

**Why this works:**
- Rewards curiosity without cluttering the card face
- Creates a sense that the world is larger than the game
- Fits the tarot register perfectly — tarot cards have always implied hidden meaning beyond their surface
- Bridges the physical and digital versions of the game into a shared lore ecosystem

**Implementation notes:**
- QR codes should be small enough to feel like part of the card's ornamental border, not bolted on
- The lore portal is a design asset, not an afterthought — plan it alongside card art, not after
- Consider: do all cards have lore entries from launch, or are some intentionally blank ("this soul has not yet spoken")?
