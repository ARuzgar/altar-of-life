# Altar of Life — Game Overview

> *"Everything has a price. Here, that price is you."*

## Concept

**Altar of Life** is a dark fantasy card game about the dead competing to return to the living. Players are souls who have arrived at a threshold between oblivion and resurrection — drawn to the Altar by unfinished business, unresolved love, or sheer refusal to disappear.

The Altar does not give. It bargains. **Your health is your currency** — the soul energy you carry from life. Bid it to gain power. Spend what remains to use that power. Die with nothing left, and there is nothing left of you.

The last soul standing wins the right to return.

## Tone

**Melodramatic-romantic.** The emotional register of a last letter, a final night, a defiance that knows it will probably fail. Beautiful, desperate, and mythic in scale. Inspired by Persona 3, Death Parade, tarot tradition, and the folklore of every culture that has ever bargained with death.

## Format

- **Tabletop card game** (primary design target)
- **Video game** (parallel development, adapted ruleset)

## Players & Modes

- **Core mode:** Multiplayer free-for-all
- **Planned modes:** Team-based, 1v1 duel (TBD)

## Win Condition

**Outlast all opponents.** The last soul with health above zero wins the right to resurrection. All others are extinguished.

## Design Pillars

1. **Every choice costs something real** — health is soul energy; spending it is irreversible sacrifice
2. **No safe turns** — the mana cap forces action; hoarding is punished
3. **Tension through asymmetry** — powerful souls are fragile; desperate souls are unpredictable
4. **Readable identity** — typed mana and card schools give each player a distinct playstyle
5. **The game reveals character** — how you bid, what you sacrifice, says something true about who you were

## Core Resources

| Resource | How Acquired | How Spent | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Health** | Starting soul energy pool | Bidding on cards, health-drain effects | Zero = permanent extinguishing |
| **Mana** | Drawn blind from mana deck each round | Playing Souls, casting Rites, activating Relics | Capped at end of round — use it or lose it |

## Card Types

| Type | Acquired Via | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Souls** | Auction | Field presence — spirits that fight for you |
| **Rites** | Auction | Spells and invocations — one-use power |
| **Relics** | Direct purchase | Persistent objects — passive and equipped effects |

*See [[Card Types]] for full definitions.*

## Shared Decks

All players draw from three communal decks:
- **Mana Deck** — blind mana draws each round
- **Souls & Rites Deck** — the market for the auction
- **Relics Deck** — available for direct purchase

## Round Structure

```
DRAW PHASE  →  ACQUISITION PHASE  →  ACTION PHASE
```

1. **Draw** — Discard unwanted cards, refill hand to 7, draw mana
2. **Acquisition** — Auction for Souls/Rites (health bid), or buy a Relic at fixed cost, or skip
3. **Action** — Spend mana to play cards, attack, and use abilities
