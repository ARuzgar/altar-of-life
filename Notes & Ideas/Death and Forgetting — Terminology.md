# Death and Forgetting — Terminology

> They are already dead. What do we call it when it happens again?

## The Problem

Standard card game language — "dies," "destroy," "graveyard," "first time you die" — doesn't work when the players and their Souls are already dead. We need an internal vocabulary that is both mechanically precise and tonally coherent with the world.

This note explores the terminology space. Nothing here is finalized.

---

## Two Subjects, Two Vocabularies

There are two distinct entities that can "die" in this game, and they should have different language:

1. **Soul cards** — spirits on the field that get destroyed in combat or by effects
2. **Players** — the dead souls competing at the Altar, who are eliminated when health hits zero

These may share some language, or be deliberately distinct. To be decided.

---

## For Soul Cards

### The Two-State System

Souls have two death states (see [[Card Types]]):

| State | Meaning | Game Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Dead** | Destroyed but recoverable | Liminal Zone |
| **Forgotten** | Permanently removed | Void / Purgatory / Chaos (TBD) |

### Language for "Destroyed" (First Death)

When a Soul is removed from the field to the Liminal Zone:

| Term | Feel |
|---|---|
| *Fades* | Soft, poetic, melancholic |
| *Dissolves* | Eerie, physical |
| *Falls silent* | Quiet, musical |
| *Is laid to rest* | Ceremonial, bittersweet |
| *Returns to the threshold* | Lore-specific, spatial |
| *Passes* | Gentle, ambiguous |

### Language for "Forgotten" (Second / Permanent Death)

When a Soul is permanently removed from the game:

| Term | Feel |
|---|---|
| *Is forgotten* | ✅ Strong — ties to the core theme of memory and identity |
| *Is extinguished* | Final, cold |
| *Is cast into the void* | Dramatic, spatial |
| *Is condemned* | Judgmental, formal |
| *Is unremembered* | Poetic variant of forgotten |
| *Passes into Chaos / Purgatory* | Lore-dependent — needs world-building to land |

**Current lean:** "Forgotten" for the permanent state, with "fades" or "falls silent" for the first death. The contrast matters — the first death is quiet and recoverable; being forgotten is absolute.

## For Players

### When a Player Reaches 0 Health

This is not death — they're already dead. It is something else. Options:

| Term | Feel |
|---|---|
| *Is forgotten* | Parallel to Soul language — powerful if unified |
| *Fades entirely* | Poetic, final |
| *Is extinguished* | Cold, mechanical |
| *Ceases* | Minimal, devastating |
| *Is silenced* | Musical, haunting |
| *Loses their echo* | If health = echo/soul energy, this lands thematically |
| *Is unwritten* | Unusual, implies their story ends mid-sentence |

### Note on Unifying Player and Soul Language

If player elimination uses the same word as Soul permanent death ("forgotten"), it creates resonance — a player who reaches 0 is experiencing what their Souls feared. This could be meaningful. Or it could be confusing. Worth arguing when the world vocabulary is more settled.

## Card Text Implications

Once terms are chosen, card text rewrites:

| Generic | Altar of Life version (draft) |
|---|---|
| "When this creature dies..." | "When this Soul fades..." |
| "The first time this creature would die..." | "The first time this Soul would fade..." |
| "Exile this creature" | "Forget this Soul" / "Condemn this Soul" |
| "Return target creature from your graveyard" | "Recall this Soul from the Liminal Zone" |
| "When a player is eliminated..." | "When a player is forgotten..." / "When a player ceases..." |

### The "Liminal Zone"

A working name for where Dead (but not Forgotten) Souls wait. Other candidates:
- *The Threshold*
- *The In-Between*
- *The Waiting Place*
- *Purgatory* (if the world uses that lore)
- *The Pale*
- *The Echo*

---

## What Causes Forgetting?

Still open — options for what moves a Soul from Dead to Forgotten:

- A specific card effect ("Condemn," "Erase," "Cast into the Void")
- Dying a second time while already in the Liminal Zone
- A player intentionally sacrificing their own Soul for a powerful effect
- A Rite that targets the Liminal Zone specifically
