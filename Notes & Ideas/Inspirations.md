## Direct Inspirations

### Persona 3 *(Game)*
The defining text for "death as a daily reality you confront rather than fear." Key touchstones:
- **Memento Mori** — the game's core philosophy; you will die, so how do you live (or unlive)?
- The Dark Hour and Tartarus as a liminal space between life and death
- Shadows as fragmented psyches, not monsters
- The tone: melancholic but never hopeless, romantic in the literary sense
- **What to take:** the emotional weight of mortality as identity, not just obstacle

### Death Parade *(Anime)*
Dead souls arrive at a bar and must play games that reveal who they truly were in life. The arbiter judges whether they are reincarnated or cast into the void.
- The game-as-judgment structure — play reveals character
- The intimacy of competition between people who don't know each other's full story
- Decim and the question of whether arbiters (or players) can truly be neutral
- **What to take:** the idea that how you play says something true about your soul. Consider: does how a player bids and uses health say something about their character in-lore?

### Tarot
The card aesthetic is the most obvious pull, but the symbolism runs deeper:
- Major Arcana as archetypes: Death (transformation, not ending), The Tower (sudden collapse), The Moon (illusion, the unconscious), The Devil (bondage by choice), The Hanged Man (suspension, sacrifice for wisdom)
- Suits as elemental schools — possibly mapped to mana types
- The idea that cards *reveal* rather than determine
- **What to take:** card names, imagery themes, the notion that the deck is a cosmology

### Folklore & Mythology
A non-exhaustive source list to mine:

| Source | Key Element |
|---|---|
| **Greek — Charon / River Styx** | You pay to cross. Health-as-toll is literally this. |
| **Egyptian — Weighing of the Heart** | The heart weighed against a feather. The soul judged by what it carried. |
| **Japanese — Shinigami** | Gods of death, not evil — bureaucratic, melancholic, sometimes sympathetic |
| **Celtic — The Wild Hunt** | The dead ride; the living risk being swept up. A hunt with no clean resolution. |
| **Slavic — Koschei the Deathless** | Immortality hidden in nested objects (egg, needle, duck) — souls bound to relics |
| **Mexican — Día de los Muertos** | Death as reunion, not ending. The dead remember and are remembered. |
| **Norse — Hel / Niflheim** | A cold, grey afterlife for those who didn't die gloriously. Quiet, not dramatic. |

## Adjacent Games (Study These)

### Death's Door *(Video Game)*
A crow reaper in a bureaucratic afterlife. Quiet, lonely, beautiful.
- **Why it's relevant:** the aesthetic of death-as-profession, not chaos. The Altar might be run by someone or something.

### KeyForge *(Card Game)*
Unique deck game — every deck is different, procedurally generated.
- **Why it's relevant:** model for how typed mana and soul schools could vary by deck composition

## Art & Aesthetic References

### Klimt — *Death and Life* (1915)
Gold leaf, decorative flatness, the literally intertwined figures of death and the living.
- The foil card aesthetic connects directly here.

### Alphonse Mucha — Art Nouveau
Flowing lines, celestial motifs, women as cosmic symbols. The ornamental border style of his posters is a clear ancestor of tarot card framing.

### Remedios Varo
Spanish surrealist — her work is full of robed figures, celestial navigation, and alchemical processes. Deeply aligned with the game's aesthetic register.

### Zdzisław Beksiński
Polish surrealist. Hauntingly beautiful, post-apocalyptic and dreamlike. Useful for darker Soul card aesthetics.

## Further Recommendations

These are personal suggestions — things that might not be obvious sources but could enrich the design:

- **The Book Thief** *(Novel)* — Death as first-person narrator. Wry, tender, weary. Consider: who or what is the "voice" of the Altar?
- **The Sandman** *(Comic/Netflix)* — Death as a cheerful goth. Dream as a world with rules and politics. Highly relevant lore model.
- **Purgatorio** *(Dante's Divine Comedy, Part II)* — souls working through something to earn return. The game loop as purgatorial.
- **Spirited Away** *(Film)* — a living person in a spirit economy, working off a debt to survive. The mana and health economy maps to this loosely.
- **Made in Abyss** *(Anime)* — descent into something beautiful and deadly, where the cost of going deeper is physical and permanent. Escalating sacrifice.
- **Violeta Parra — "Gracias a la vida"** *(Song)* — a woman thanking life as she prepares to leave it. Captures the melodramatic-romantic tone perfectly.

## Tone Calibration

The target tone sits in a specific quadrant. It is:

- **Romantic** — not in the Valentine's Day sense, but in the literary sense: heightened emotion, dramatic stakes, beauty in suffering
- **Melodramatic** — feelings are large and valid; no ironic distance
- **Melancholic** — not grimdark, not hopeless. There is something worth fighting for.
- **Mythic** — the world has weight and history; the dead are not random, they are chosen or fated

It is **not:** edgy for its own sake, nihilistic, comedic, or detached.
