# The Altar — Nature & Implementation

> asterisk — revisit later. Not ready to commit. Needs deeper thought on tabletop AND multiplayer video game implementation before any decisions.

## The Core Question

What *is* the Altar? Is it a character, a machine, a presence, a remnant? This shapes the tone of every game session, the lore spine, and potentially the mechanics.

## Four Options on the Table

### Option 1: Sentient Arbiter
A being with intent. Watches every game. Set the rules or chooses to enforce them. Could favor souls, despise them, or be rigidly neutral.
- Rich for video game narrative — becomes a character
- Risk: steals focus from the players

### Option 2: Ancient Machine
No name, no face, no intent. It processes. Rules exist because they always have. No one knows who built it.
- Pure dread, impersonal
- Risk: too cold for the melodramatic-romantic tone

### Option 3: The Dimmed Entity
Was once a vast consciousness. Has been running so long its awareness has faded to reflex and pattern. Mostly machine now — but something occasionally flickers. A pause, a word that almost means something, a market that produces exactly the card a grieving soul needed.
- Most tonally aligned — melancholic, vast, possibly pitiable
- Mystery that never fully resolves
- Risk: vague if not implemented carefully

### Option 4: The Altar Is the Forgotten
Every permanently eliminated soul didn't disappear — they became part of the Altar. It runs on them. It *is* them. Its behavior is the aggregate weight of all past human desperation, which is why it almost makes sense.
- Darkest and most thematically resonant option
- Reframes the whole game — winning means surviving something made of losers
- Being Forgotten means joining it
- Risk: heavy revelation that needs careful handling in both formats

## Altar Events ⭐

Regardless of the Altar's nature, **Altar Events** is a strong mechanic concept — moments where the Altar itself does something. A card added to the market. A rule that shifts for a round. Something that feels almost intentional.

These could be:
- A dedicated Altar Events deck drawn from at set intervals
- Triggered by specific game states (someone reaching low health, a Soul being Forgotten, etc.)
- Random but patterned — players can't quite crack the logic

**This idea survives whichever nature option we choose.** Even a machine has outputs. Even a dimmed entity has reflexes. Even the weight of the Forgotten has patterns.

## What Needs Answering Before We Decide

- How does the Altar's nature manifest differently at a tabletop vs. a multiplayer video game?
- In tabletop: who or what "plays" the Altar? Is it the game itself, a card deck, a board state?
- In video game: is it environmental storytelling, a character, an emergent system?
- Does the Altar need to be the same thing in both formats, or can they interpret it differently?
