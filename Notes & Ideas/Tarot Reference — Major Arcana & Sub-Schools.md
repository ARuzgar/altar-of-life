# Tarot Reference — Major Arcana & Sub-Schools

> Reference document only. No design decisions made here. This is the landscape to understand before we choose.

---

## The Major Arcana

The Major Arcana are the 22 trump cards of a tarot deck. They represent archetypal forces, significant life themes, and the larger patterns that shape existence. In a reading, they carry more weight than the Minor Arcana — they signal something that matters, something beyond the everyday. They are numbered 0–21 and follow a loose narrative arc sometimes called **The Fool's Journey** — a soul moving through experience toward wholeness.

---

### 0 — The Fool

**Core meaning:** Beginnings, innocence, spontaneity, a leap into the unknown.

**Interpretation:** The Fool stands at the edge of a cliff, about to step off — not from ignorance but from trust. He carries everything he owns in a small bag. He doesn't know what comes next and isn't afraid of that. The Fool is pure potential, the moment before the story begins. In some readings, he represents divine madness — the wisdom of not knowing.

**Shadow side:** Recklessness, naivety, ignoring real danger, refusing to grow up.

**Feel:** Light, open, dangerous in a beautiful way. The Fool is the card of the person who jumps.

---

### I — The Magician

**Core meaning:** Will, skill, resourcefulness, manifestation, making something from nothing.

**Interpretation:** The Magician has all four suits of the tarot on his table — cups, swords, wands, pentacles — and the tools to use them. He points one hand to the sky, one to the earth: as above, so below. He transforms intention into reality. He is the first act of deliberate creation.

**Shadow side:** Manipulation, trickery, using skill for deception, the con artist.

**Feel:** Sharp, confident, controlled. The Magician is power that knows what it's doing.

---

### II — The High Priestess

**Core meaning:** Intuition, mystery, the unconscious, hidden knowledge, the veil between worlds.

**Interpretation:** The High Priestess sits between two pillars — one light, one dark — and behind her hangs a veil decorated with pomegranates. She holds a scroll she doesn't show you. She knows things she won't say. She represents the knowledge that can't be spoken, only felt — the intuition that arrives before you understand why.

**Shadow side:** Secrets becoming isolation, knowledge hoarded, being so interior you lose connection to reality.

**Feel:** Still, deep, withheld. The High Priestess is the pause before the answer.

---

### III — The Empress

**Core meaning:** Abundance, fertility, nature, nurturing, creation, sensory pleasure.

**Interpretation:** The Empress is seated in a lush garden, crowned with stars, robed in pomegranate patterns. She is generative — everything grows around her. She represents the creative force of the natural world, the mother in the mythic sense, the body and its pleasures, the cycle of growth and harvest.

**Shadow side:** Smothering, overabundance, dependency, refusing to let things die when their time has come.

**Feel:** Rich, embodied, warm. The Empress is life being enthusiastically alive.

---

### IV — The Emperor

**Core meaning:** Structure, authority, order, stability, the father, rulership.

**Interpretation:** The Emperor sits on a stone throne, armored, holding a scepter. The landscape behind him is barren — he has imposed order on the wilderness. He represents law, hierarchy, the systems that make civilization possible, the force that says *this is how things are done*.

**Shadow side:** Rigidity, authoritarianism, control for its own sake, refusing to bend when bending is wisdom.

**Feel:** Cold, hard, stable. The Emperor is the weight of institutions.

---

### V — The Hierophant

**Core meaning:** Tradition, institutions, spiritual authority, conformity, the transmission of wisdom.

**Interpretation:** The Hierophant is the Pope — the religious institution made human. He passes down received wisdom, initiates people into traditions, blesses and consecrates. He represents the value of ritual and community and the structures that carry knowledge across generations.

**Shadow side:** Dogma, rigidity, punishment for deviation, using tradition as a cage.

**Feel:** Formal, ceremonial, heavy with history. The Hierophant is the weight of what came before.

---

### VI — The Lovers

**Core meaning:** Love, choice, alignment, union, values, the decisive fork in the road.

**Interpretation:** Despite the name, The Lovers is as much about choice as it is about love. An angel presides over two figures who must choose. In older decks this was literally a man choosing between two women. The deeper meaning is: what do you value enough to commit to? What do you choose when you cannot have everything?

**Shadow side:** Indecision, bad choices made from desire rather than wisdom, relationships built on shaky foundations.

**Feel:** Tender, tense, electric. The Lovers is the moment before the irreversible choice.

---

### VII — The Chariot

**Core meaning:** Willpower, victory through control, determination, forward motion, mastery of opposing forces.

**Interpretation:** The Charioteer is armored, poised, controlling two sphinxes pulling in different directions. He doesn't use reins — he holds them together through sheer will. The Chariot is about succeeding not because conditions are favorable but because you refused to let them stop you.

**Shadow side:** Aggression, forcing outcomes, running over everything in your path, winning the battle and losing everything else.

**Feel:** Fierce, kinetic, tightly controlled. The Chariot is determination as a physical force.

---

### VIII — Strength

**Core meaning:** Inner strength, courage, patience, compassion over brute force, taming the wild.

**Interpretation:** A figure — usually depicted as gentle, often feminine — holds the jaws of a lion open or closed, not by force but by calm. The lion is not defeated; it is understood. Strength here is not domination but mastery through gentleness. It is the long courage, not the explosive kind.

**Shadow side:** Self-doubt, weakness disguised as humility, failing to act when action is needed.

**Feel:** Quiet, deep, patient. Strength is the card of the long game.

---

### IX — The Hermit

**Core meaning:** Solitude, introspection, withdrawal, inner guidance, the long walk alone.

**Interpretation:** The Hermit stands on a mountain peak in a gray robe, holding a lantern and a staff. He has withdrawn from the world not to escape it but to see it more clearly. He is the guide who has already walked the path — the lamp he carries is for others, not himself.

**Shadow side:** Isolation become permanent, withdrawal become avoidance, wisdom that never returns to share itself.

**Feel:** Sparse, cold, clarifying. The Hermit is the silence after everything else falls away.

---

### X — Wheel of Fortune

**Core meaning:** Fate, cycles, turning points, chance, the forces larger than any individual.

**Interpretation:** The Wheel turns. Figures rise and fall on it — some ascending, some descending, none in control of the turning itself. The Wheel of Fortune represents the larger rhythms of existence: that things change, that luck shifts, that what rises will fall and what has fallen may rise. No position on the wheel is permanent.

**Shadow side:** Fatalism, using fate as an excuse for passivity, refusing to act because "it was meant to be."

**Feel:** Vast, indifferent, cyclical. The Wheel is bigger than you.

---

### XI — Justice

**Core meaning:** Truth, fairness, cause and effect, accountability, the law of consequence.

**Interpretation:** Justice sits between two pillars (like the High Priestess), holding scales and a sword. She sees clearly and decides clearly. She is not mercy — she is accuracy. What you have done will be weighed. The consequences will match the action. She is not cruel; she is exact.

**Shadow side:** Rigidity that ignores context, punishment without understanding, using the appearance of fairness to disguise unfairness.

**Feel:** Clear, cold, inevitable. Justice is the moment the bill comes due.

---

### XII — The Hanged Man

**Core meaning:** Suspension, surrender, sacrifice, a different perspective, waiting, wisdom through voluntary stillness.

**Interpretation:** A figure hangs upside-down from one foot, serene. He is not being punished — he chose this. By surrendering control, by giving up his normal orientation to the world, he sees something others cannot. The Hanged Man is the card of the pause that changes everything, the sacrifice that opens a door.

**Shadow side:** Martyrdom as identity, refusing to move even when movement is possible, using "waiting" to avoid deciding.

**Feel:** Strange, suspended, luminous. The Hanged Man is the wisdom that only comes from letting go.

---

### XIII — Death

**Core meaning:** Ending, transformation, transition, the clearing that makes space for the new.

**Interpretation:** Death rides a white horse. A king lies at his feet. A child offers him flowers. A priest pleads. None of it stops him. But Death in the tarot is not annihilation — it is change so complete that what came before cannot continue. Something ends entirely so something else can begin. It is the most misunderstood card in the deck.

**Shadow side:** Resistance to necessary endings, clinging to what has already died, letting fear of change prevent transformation.

**Feel:** Absolute, quiet, unstoppable. Death is the one appointment no one misses.

---

### XIV — Temperance

**Core meaning:** Balance, moderation, patience, alchemy, blending opposites into something new.

**Interpretation:** An angelic figure pours water between two cups, one high, one low — endlessly, perfectly. She stands with one foot on land and one in water. She is the card of synthesis: not choosing between extremes but finding what they become when mixed. Temperance is the long, patient work of integration.

**Shadow side:** Endless compromise that commits to nothing, moderation as avoidance of passion, the blandness of always splitting the difference.

**Feel:** Gentle, slow, alchemical. Temperance is the work that takes years.

---

### XV — The Devil

**Core meaning:** Bondage, temptation, materialism, the chains you chose and can choose to remove.

**Interpretation:** The Devil presides over two figures chained to his pedestal — but the chains are loose. They could remove them. They don't. The Devil is not an external force of evil; he is the things we bind ourselves to willingly. Addiction, comfort, the familiar cage. He is also vitality, pleasure, the body's appetites — not inherently evil, but dangerous when they own you.

**Shadow side:** Is the shadow side. Though its inverse is reclaiming what the Devil represents: desire, power, earthly reality, on your own terms.

**Feel:** Dark, seductive, honest. The Devil is the card of what we don't want to admit we want.

---

### XVI — The Tower

**Core meaning:** Sudden upheaval, revelation through destruction, the collapse of false structures, lightning.

**Interpretation:** Lightning strikes a tower. Figures fall from windows. The crown flies off the top. The Tower is the moment everything you built on a false foundation comes down — violently, completely, without warning. This is terrifying and also necessary. You cannot build what is real until what is false has been destroyed.

**Shadow side:** Chaos for its own sake, destruction without purpose, the inability to rebuild after collapse.

**Feel:** Violent, sudden, clarifying in retrospect. The Tower is the disaster that was always coming.

---

### XVII — The Star

**Core meaning:** Hope, renewal, healing, guidance, light after darkness, the promise that continues.

**Interpretation:** A naked figure kneels by a pool under a sky of stars, pouring water onto land and into the water. She is completely at ease. The Star follows The Tower — it is what remains when everything false has fallen. It is not triumphant hope but quiet hope, the kind that doesn't need to announce itself. The world is still here. There is still water. The stars still give light.

**Shadow side:** False hope, wishful thinking, refusing to acknowledge that the stars don't fix anything on their own.

**Feel:** Still, open, tender. The Star is hope without performance.

---

### XVIII — The Moon

**Core meaning:** Illusion, the unconscious, fear, dreams, the hidden self, the space between knowing and not knowing.

**Interpretation:** A moon face gazes down on a landscape where a dog and a wolf howl at it, and a crayfish emerges from a pool. A path leads between two towers into the unknown. The Moon illuminates without clarifying — everything is visible but nothing is certain. She is the card of the unconscious mind, the dream logic, the fear that walks with you down the dark road.

**Shadow side:** Being lost in illusion, anxiety, paranoia, the refusal to look at what the moonlight is actually showing.

**Feel:** Uneasy, beautiful, uncertain. The Moon is the card of the 3am thought you can't unhave.

---

### XIX — The Sun

**Core meaning:** Joy, vitality, clarity, success, the warmth of being fully alive and seen.

**Interpretation:** A child rides a white horse under an enormous sun, arms open, sunflowers behind them. The Sun is the card of simple, total joy — the pleasure of existing, of being warm, of things working. It is not complex. It doesn't need to be. After all the darkness of the major arcana, the Sun is the moment you step outside and everything is fine.

**Shadow side:** Naivety, the blindness of too much light, the parts of reality you can't see because you're looking directly at the Sun.

**Feel:** Warm, open, uncomplicated. The Sun is the card of the good day.

---

### XX — Judgement

**Core meaning:** Reckoning, absolution, resurrection, the call to rise, the moment of final accounting.

**Interpretation:** An angel blows a trumpet. The dead rise from their coffins. They are being called — not condemned, but summoned. Judgement is the moment everything is seen clearly, every action weighed, every life accounted for — and then the call: *rise*. It is resurrection as reckoning, return as revelation.

**Shadow side:** Self-condemnation, the inability to forgive yourself and rise, being so focused on past judgment that you can't answer the call forward.

**Feel:** Vast, solemn, transformative. Judgement is the moment you are finally, fully seen.

---

### XXI — The World

**Core meaning:** Completion, integration, wholeness, the end of a cycle, the fullness of having arrived.

**Interpretation:** A dancing figure is encircled by a laurel wreath, surrounded by four figures at the corners — the same four that appear on The Wheel of Fortune. She holds two wands. She has completed the Fool's Journey. The World is not a destination but a state — the experience of wholeness, of having fully inhabited your existence. Then the Fool steps off the cliff again and it begins anew.

**Shadow side:** Stagnation, clinging to completion, refusing the next beginning because this one felt so good.

**Feel:** Full, still, complete. The World is the exhale at the end of a very long journey.

## Psychological Sub-Schools — Full Candidate List

Emotional and psychological states to draw from when assigning thematic flavor to cards and schools. This list is deliberately wide — not all of these will be used.

### Loss & Grief
Grief, Mourning, Longing, Yearning, Nostalgia, Absence, Hollowness, Bereavement, Ache

### Fear & Anxiety
Dread, Terror, Paranoia, Unease, Foreboding, Panic, Vertigo, Suspicion

### Anger & Defiance
Rage, Fury, Defiance, Spite, Resentment, Bitterness, Indignation, Wrath, Vengeance

### Love & Connection
Love, Devotion, Tenderness, Longing, Adoration, Attachment, Obsession, Possession

### Acceptance & Peace
Acceptance, Surrender, Peace, Stillness, Release, Resignation, Serenity, Equanimity

### Hope & Will
Hope, Determination, Resolve, Ambition, Faith, Perseverance, Defiance (of despair)

### Guilt & Shame
Guilt, Shame, Remorse, Regret, Self-reproach, Unworthiness, Contrition

### Pride & Identity
Pride, Dignity, Honor, Vanity, Arrogance, Ego, Legacy, Recognition

### Sacrifice & Suffering
Sacrifice, Martyrdom, Endurance, Suffering, Bearing, The long cost

### Wonder & Mystery
Awe, Wonder, Curiosity, Mystery, The uncanny, Reverence

### Isolation
Solitude, Loneliness, Withdrawal, Exile, Abandonment, The weight of being unseen

### Transformation & Becoming
Becoming, Metamorphosis, Dissolution, Emergence, Shedding, Threshold-crossing
